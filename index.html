Reading this dashboard▸
Date windows

Trailing 7 Days — the 7 days ending yesterday (rolling window, not a calendar week).

MTD (month to date) — the 1st of the current month through the same end date as the trailing 7-day window (yesterday), so all three Snapshot windows close on the same day.

QTD (quarter to date) — the 1st of the current quarter through that same end date.

Reading the deltas

Snapshot Δ — change vs a fixed Jan–Apr 2026 historical benchmark, not the prior period and not a rolling window — this baseline stays Jan–Apr going forward regardless of when the dashboard is pulled. Rate metrics (engagement rate, session time, organic share) compare directly to the Jan–Apr average; volume metrics (impressions, shares, sessions, etc.) compare to the benchmark's average daily pace scaled to the window length. This answers “am I beating my historical run-rate” rather than “did I beat last week.”

Trend headline — latest complete month (June 2026) vs the prior month.

Final numbers tab — totals for the period that just closed: completed June and completed Q2, each measured against the same fixed Jan–Apr 2026 benchmark as the Snapshot tab (scaled to 30 days for June, 91 days for Q2), not vs the prior month/quarter.

Benchmarks

Benchmark band — on each primary & secondary line chart, the shaded magenta band is the typical monthly range for 2026, derived from the fixed Jan–Apr 2026 baseline as the mean ± 1 standard deviation; the dashed line is the mean. This is the same benchmark used for the Snapshot Δs above, just shown at monthly resolution. A point inside the band is a normal month; above the band is a strong month, below is a soft one. Total Followers (cumulative) and Video Views (unreliable in Sprout) have no band.

Current Snapshot
How to read Δ: every change below is measured against a fixed Jan–Apr 2026 historical benchmark — not the prior week/month/quarter, and not a rolling window. It's a run-rate check against that fixed baseline: is this period pacing ahead of or behind our early-2026 typical performance.
Primary KPIs — LinkedIn (post-level · Post Performance)
Primary KPI definitions▸

Engagement Rate — engagements ÷ impressions on posts published in the period; how well content resonates relative to how many saw it.

Content Reach — total impressions on posts published in the period; how far content traveled.

Stickiness — Shares — times posts were reshared (amplification).

Stickiness — Comments — comments on posts (conversation).

Stickiness — Saves — times posts were saved; not a LinkedIn metric, so it reads 0.

Video Views — views of video posts; currently unreliable in Sprout (known data issue).

Secondary KPIs (profile-level · LinkedIn Pages)
Secondary KPI definitions▸

Profile Impressions — impressions on the Pindrop LinkedIn Page itself — brand interest beyond any single post.

Channel Growth — net new followers (gained minus lost) during the period.

Total Followers — cumulative LinkedIn Page follower count.

Traffic to Site — Organic Social + Organic Video (GA4)
Traffic KPI definitions▸

Sessions — visits to the site arriving from organic social and organic video sources.

Total / New Users — unique visitors, and the first-time subset, from those sources.

Engagement Rate (site) — share of sessions that were engaged (lasted 10s+, converted, or had 2+ pageviews).

Session Time — average session duration.

Organic Share — organic (social + video) sessions as a percent of all site sessions.

Traffic note: since Q3 began Jul 1, MTD and QTD are the same window (Jul 1–9) this week; they'll diverge as the quarter progresses.
Organic Social + Organic Video — share of total sessions
